HOW TO PLAY

In blackjack, the player faces off against the dealer. The primary goal for the player is to reach a total as close to 21 as possible without exceeding it, while the dealer must draw another card (HIT) if their hand value is less than 17. The player has two main choices: HIT (request another card) or STAY (keep the current hand). As long as the player's total value remains under 21, they have the option to HIT for additional cards or choose to STAY if they feel their hand is strong enough to beat the dealer.

Special Moves:

  • Doubling Down: If the player has only two cards and hasn’t HIT for a third card, they may have the option to double down. Doubling down is available only if the player's current balance matches their initial bet. When a player doubles down, they receive one additional card, and their bet size doubles.
  • Splitting Cards: Unfortunately, there isn't a feature to split cards of the same value in the current version. This feature might be added in future updates.

    • Card Values:

  • Cards from 2 to 10 hold their face value.
  • Jacks (J), Queens (Q), and Kings (K) are each worth 10.
  • Aces (A) can be valued as either 1 or 11, depending on the other cards in the hand to optimize the total hand value.
